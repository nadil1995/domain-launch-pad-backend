Opening: Italian Game - Giuoco Piano

1. e4 - White starts with the most popular opening move
2. e5 - Black establishes a symmetrical position
3. Nf3 - Developing with a threat to e5
4. Nc6 - Natural development while defending e5
5. Bc4 - The Italian Game begins! Targeting f7
6. Bc5 - Black develops the light-squared bishop symmetrically
7. c3 - White prepares d4 to fight for the center
8. Nf6 - Black attacks e4 and completes kingside development
9. d4 - White strikes in the center
10. exd4 - Black accepts the challenge
11. cxd4 - White has a strong pawn center
12. Bb4+ - Black gives check to disrupt White's smooth development

The Italian Game leads to open, tactical positions where both sides have active pieces.
